P-Series

What is a P-Series?

In calculus, the p-series is a type of infinite series of the form:

Î£ n=1 ∞1/n^p

where p is a constant greater than 0. The p-series is an important example in the study of infinite series, because its convergence behavior depends on the value of p. If p is greater than 1, the p-series converges, which means that the sum of its terms approaches a finite value as the number of terms increases. If p is less than or equal to 1, the p-series diverges, which means that the sum of its terms does not approach a finite value as the number of terms increases.
