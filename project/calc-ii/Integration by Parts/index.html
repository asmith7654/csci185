Integration by Parts (Tabular Method)

What is Integration by Parts (Tabular Method)?

Integration by parts is a technique used to evaluate the integral of a product of two functions, u(x) and v(x). The basic idea behind the tabular method is to create a table of values for u(x), v(x), and their integrals/derivatives that can be used to simplify the integration process.

How to use Integration by Parts (Tabular Method)

  1. Look for a product of two functions in the integrand (be mindful that divison/fractions can oftentimes be rewritten in terms of multiplication).
  2. Create a table with three columns: a sign column, a derivative column, and an integral column.
  3. In the sign column, input alternating signs down the column starting with a positive sign in the head of the column.
  4. Assign each function to either the derivative column or the integral column. Aim to put easily differentiable functions in the derivative column, and easily integratable functions in the integral column.
  5. Start filling out the table. There are three conditions that can be met to stop filling out the table:

    One of the rows contains a zero.

    You can find the integral of the product of the row.

    The product of the row is a multiple of the original equation.

  6. If one of the rows contain a zero, add the diagonal products of all the rows. That is your final answer.
  7. If you can find the integral of the product of the last row, add the diagonal products of all the prior rows, and integrate the product of the last row. Afterwards, add that integral to your current summation. That is your final answer.
  8. If the product of the last row is a multiple of the original equation, add the diagonal products of all the prior rows, and include the integral of the last row at the end. Set your summation equal to the original equation you're integrating, and add or subtract your multiple to the original equation accordingly. Finally, you must return the integral to its original form, so multiply by the reciprocal of the integrals coefficient on both sides of the equation. That is your final answer.
