Divergence Test

What is the Divergence Test?

The divergence test, also known as the nth term test for divergence, is a method used to determine whether an infinite series converges or diverges. The test states that if the limit of the nth term of a series as n approaches infinity is not equal to zero, then the series diverges.

In other words, if the limit of the an is not equal to zero, then the series does not converge. However, it is important to note that the test only tells us that the series diverges; if the limit is equal to zero, no information is obtained.
