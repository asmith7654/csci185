Integral Test

What is the Integral Test?

The integral test is a method used in calculus to determine the convergence or divergence of an infinite series. It involves comparing the infinite series with an improper integral.

The integral test states that if f(x) is a continuous, positive, and decreasing function, and if an = f(n), then the series sum an converges if and only if the integral from 1 to infinity of f(x) dx converges.

In other words, if the improper integral from 1 to infinity of f(x) dx converges, then the series sum an also converges, and if the improper integral diverges, then the series sum an also diverges.

How to use the Integral Test

  1. Look for series whose an is integratable.
  2. Prove that the function is continuous, positive, and decreasing. To prove it is decreasing, you can take the first derivative and find whether it is negative.
  3. If the function met the prior criteria, create a matching improper integral that approaches infinity.
  4. Integrate and evaluate. If the sum is a singular finite value, the integral converges. If the sum is infinite or oscillates, the integral diverges. By the Integral Test, the original series diverges/converges by your findings.
